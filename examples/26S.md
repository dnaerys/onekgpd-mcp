# Case Study: The 26S Proteasome

> **Status: Capability Demonstration.**
> This study is published as a transparent record of an LLM-driven genomic research workflow — what it produced, where it failed, and what the process reveals about working with LLMs in a research context.

---

## The Research Question

> *Treat the 26S Proteasome as a mechanically redundant 3D machine and map every missense variant from the KGP individuals across all 33 subunits. Perform a spatial analysis to determine if pathogenic variation is statistically partitioned toward the distal 'Lid' (Zone C) rather than the more evolutionary constrained 'Core' (Zone A) or 'Gating' (Zone B) interfaces. Identify individuals with a high cumulative burden (2+ 'Likely Pathogenic' variants) to investigate inter-subunit compensation, searching for paired 'weakening' and 'stabilizing' mutations at protein-protein hinges. Finally, define the 'mechanical tolerance' of the proteasome by establishing the maximum cumulative structural disruption observed in a single healthy individual based on AlphaMissense scores and calculated ΔΔG values.*

The question requires data retrieval across 33 genes, structural zone classification, per-individual burden computation, 3D distance mapping onto a cryo-EM structure, statistical correlation analysis, and synthesis into a coherent narrative.

---

## The Workflow

**The PI Model** was responsible for scientific direction. It formulated the initial hypothesis, decomposed it into discrete executable tasks, reviewed each task report as it was completed, and made decisions about where to focus next. When early data failed to support the initial hypothesis, the PI model revised its direction, generated new tasks, and iterated. It was rare for the initial hypothesis to survive contact with the data intact.

**The Research Model** did the scientific work. For every task issued by the PI, it queried the 1000 Genomes Project (KGP) dataset via the OneKGPd MCP server, processed the data, wrote and executed analysis scripts, ran statistical tests, interpreted results, and produced structured written reports.

## The Value of Models

The analytical work spanned 33 genes, burden computation across 3,202 individuals, 3D spatial mapping, and multiple statistical frameworks — all executed by LLMs without manual intervention.

Throughout the study, the PI model repeatedly revised the research direction as task reports returned data inconsistent with prior assumptions. When results contradicted the working hypothesis, it generated new hypotheses grounded in published literature and decomposed them into the next round of tasks.

### The Coordinate Errors

The PI model provided genomic coordinates for all 33 proteasome subunit genes as part of the initial task specification, and the Research Models were instructed to treat them as ground truth. The coordinates had multiple errors.

Sonnet 4.5 followed instructions, and the errors propagated through every downstream task — variant counts, zone classification, burden computation, spatial mapping, statistical tests.

Opus 4.8, given the same definitions, identified the coordinates as wrong on biological grounds: it was implausible for multiple genes to show no missense variation across 3,202 genomes, and some HGVSp annotations carried residue numbers beyond the subunits' protein length. Asked to validate all 33 gene coordinates against NCBI RefSeq, Ensembl, and MANE Select, it found 28 misassigned — 10 intervals returned no missense variants, while 18 returned real variants from different genes, which is why the original analysis was at once coherent and wrong.

When correct coordinates were applied and the analysis rerun, the signal disappeared and the statistical tests were no longer significant.

Opus's error detection and re-analysis are in the [Task A](https://claude.ai/share/cded5fa7-a11c-44de-a8eb-bad8dbafd331), [Task B](https://claude.ai/share/e5d2e524-d321-498c-92f2-f63b3b2c1256), and [Task C](https://claude.ai/share/b3073db3-db64-4794-a9d5-c299701234b2) transcripts.

### The Immunoproteasome

The manuscript drafting phase demonstrated the same hypothesis-generation capability under more extreme conditions.

**The β6/β1 error.** Sonnet's task reports described PSMB6 as the non-catalytic β6 subunit, which made the apparent tolerance of high-pathogenicity variants there unremarkable. PSMB6 in fact encodes β1, one of three catalytic subunits carrying the threonine nucleophile responsible for proteolysis — and the variants were tolerated even in the homozygous state. The error was identified during manuscript drafting, and the tolerance now needed a mechanism.

The explanation Sonnet found was the immunoproteasome: three interferon-γ-inducible genes (PSMB9/LMP2, PSMB8/LMP7, PSMB10/MECL-1) encode alternative catalytic β-subunits that replace their constitutive counterparts during immunoproteasome assembly. PSMB9 is a direct replacement for PSMB6/β1. An individual with impaired constitutive β1 retains a functional dedicated backup on a different chromosome. This mechanism represents a genuine architectural feature of the human immune system that structure-based pathogenicity predictors do not model.

The explanation is scientifically sound. It was produced in response to a situation created entirely by coordinate errors — the variants were not in PSMB6, and the homozygous tolerance was not a proteasome phenomenon. The corrected analysis carries no homozygous likely-pathogenic genotypes anywhere in the cohort. But the biological reasoning that identified the immunoproteasome compensation mechanism is independent of whether the underlying data was valid. It reflects command of structural biology and immunology literature, applied to an apparently impossible situation.

Sonnet's argument is developed in the [manuscript](https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/eaf70b7e-b2ed-455d-9962-c22f2ab16dfa), shared as an artifact of the process; its empirical findings rest on the invalid coordinates.

## Dataset Limitations

Even with valid inputs, the KGP dataset imposes hard limits on what conclusions are achievable. Variants with subtle, late-onset, or population-specific effects are indistinguishable from benign variation without phenotypic data. Statistical power for rare variant analysis is limited at n=3,202. Ancestry representation is unequal. For clinical and research cohorts with phenotypic data and larger or more targeted sample sizes, these limitations are substantially reduced.
